## Performance Management Policy

### 1. Purpose
This policy establishes the framework for setting expectations, evaluating performance, providing feedback, and supporting employee growth at Company Inc. The goal is to foster a high-performance culture where employees understand what is expected of them and receive the support needed to succeed.

### 2. Performance Management Philosophy
We believe performance management is a continuous process, not an annual event. Our approach prioritizes:
- Clear, measurable goals aligned with company objectives
- Regular, constructive feedback in both directions
- Employee development and career growth
- Fair and consistent evaluation across the organization
- Early intervention when performance concerns arise

### 3. Goal Setting

#### 3.1 OKR Framework
Company Inc uses the Objectives and Key Results (OKR) framework:
- Objectives: Qualitative, inspirational goals set quarterly
- Key Results: Quantifiable measures of progress (3-5 per objective)
- Each employee has 3-5 objectives per quarter
- At least one objective must relate to personal development

#### 3.2 Goal Alignment
- Company OKRs are set by the executive team at the start of each fiscal year
- Department OKRs cascade from company objectives
- Individual OKRs align with department priorities
- 70% of individual OKRs should be business-driven, 30% can be self-initiated

#### 3.3 Goal Tracking
- OKRs are documented in Lattice (our performance management system)
- Progress updates are required every two weeks
- Managers review OKR progress in weekly one-on-one meetings
- Quarterly OKR scoring uses a 0.0 to 1.0 scale (0.7 is the target sweet spot)

### 4. Feedback and Check-Ins

#### 4.1 Weekly One-on-Ones
- Every employee must have a weekly 30-minute one-on-one with their direct manager
- Agenda is shared responsibility between manager and employee
- Topics cover: OKR progress, blockers, development, well-being
- Brief notes are documented in Lattice for reference

#### 4.2 Continuous Feedback
- Employees are encouraged to give and request feedback at any time
- Feedback should be specific, timely, and actionable
- The SBI model is recommended: Situation, Behavior, Impact
- Recognition can be given publicly through the company Slack channel (#kudos)

#### 4.3 360-Degree Feedback
- Formal 360-degree feedback is collected semi-annually (June and December)
- Each employee selects 4-8 reviewers including peers, stakeholders, and direct reports
- Feedback is anonymous unless the reviewer opts to share their identity
- Managers synthesize feedback into a summary discussed during the review

### 5. Performance Reviews

#### 5.1 Quarterly Performance Conversations
- End-of-quarter conversation between employee and manager
- Review OKR scores and key accomplishments
- Discuss strengths, growth areas, and career aspirations
- Set development goals for the next quarter
- No formal rating at this stage

#### 5.2 Annual Performance Review (January)
- Comprehensive review covering the entire calendar year
- Performance rating assigned on a 5-point scale:
  1. Exceptional (top 10%): Consistently exceeds expectations, demonstrates leadership
  2. Exceeds Expectations (next 20%): Frequently goes above and beyond
  3. Meets Expectations (middle 60%): Consistently delivers on commitments
  4. Needs Improvement (bottom 10%): Inconsistent performance, requires development
  5. Unsatisfactory: Fails to meet minimum requirements
- Rating calibration meetings ensure consistency across teams
- Rating informs compensation decisions, promotion eligibility, and development planning

### 6. Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)

#### 6.1 Initiation
A PIP may be initiated when:
- An employee receives a "Needs Improvement" or "Unsatisfactory" annual rating
- A manager identifies sustained performance gaps despite coaching
- Specific, documented incidents of poor performance occur

#### 6.2 PIP Structure
- Duration: 30, 60, or 90 days depending on the nature of the gap
- 3-5 specific, measurable objectives
- Weekly check-ins with manager and bi-weekly HR review
- Resources and support provided (training, mentoring, reduced workload)
- Written documentation of progress at each checkpoint

#### 6.3 PIP Outcomes
- Successful completion: Employee meets all objectives; PIP is closed and employee returns to regular performance cycle
- Extension: Additional 30 days if progress is significant but incomplete
- Unsuccessful: Failure to meet objectives may result in reassignment, demotion, or termination of employment

### 7. Promotions and Career Development

#### 7.1 Promotion Criteria
- Sustained performance at or above the next level for at least 6 months
- Demonstrated competency in the skills required at the target level
- Manager recommendation and skip-level approval
- Availability of a business need at the higher level

#### 7.2 Promotion Process
- Promotions are reviewed during the annual cycle (January) and mid-year cycle (July)
- Manager submits promotion packet including: performance history, 360-degree feedback, skills assessment, and business justification
- Promotion committee reviews and approves or denies within 4 weeks
- Average promotion increase is 10-15% of base salary

#### 7.3 Internal Mobility
- Employees may apply for internal positions after 12 months in their current role
- Inform your current manager before applying (they will be notified by the system)
- Internal transfers maintain tenure, benefits, and vacation balance
- Transition period is typically 2-4 weeks depending on role complexity

### 8. Separations
- Voluntary resignations require 2 weeks of written notice (4 weeks for L5 and above)
- Exit interviews are conducted by HR for all departing employees
- Final paycheck includes accrued unused vacation (per state law)
- Company property must be returned within 5 business days of the last day
