## Leave and Attendance Policy

### 1. Purpose
This policy establishes guidelines for employee leave, attendance, and time-off management at Company Inc. It ensures fair and consistent treatment across all departments while maintaining operational efficiency.

### 2. Scope
This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees of Company Inc. Contractors and interns are covered under separate agreements.

### 3. Types of Leave

#### 3.1 Annual Leave
- Full-time employees accrue 20 days of paid annual leave per calendar year
- Part-time employees accrue leave on a pro-rata basis based on hours worked
- Leave accrues at 1.67 days per month of service
- Up to 5 unused days may be carried forward to the next calendar year with manager approval
- Annual leave requests must be submitted at least 2 weeks in advance via the HR portal

#### 3.2 Sick Leave
- Employees receive 10 days of paid sick leave per calendar year
- Sick leave can be used for personal illness, injury, or medical appointments
- A medical certificate is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive working days
- Unused sick leave does not carry forward to the next year
- Employees must notify their manager by 9:00 AM on the day of absence

#### 3.3 Public Holidays
Company Inc observes the following public holidays:
- New Year's Day (January 1)
- Memorial Day (Last Monday in May)
- Independence Day (July 4)
- Labor Day (First Monday in September)
- Thanksgiving Day and the following Friday
- Christmas Day (December 25)
- Two floating holidays per year, subject to manager approval

#### 3.4 Parental Leave
- Primary caregivers: 16 weeks of fully paid leave
- Secondary caregivers: 4 weeks of fully paid leave
- Leave can be taken any time within the first 12 months after birth or adoption
- Employees must have completed at least 6 months of service to qualify

#### 3.5 Bereavement Leave
- Immediate family (spouse, child, parent, sibling): 5 days paid leave
- Extended family (grandparent, in-law, aunt, uncle): 3 days paid leave
- Additional unpaid leave may be requested for travel requirements

#### 3.6 Unpaid Leave
- Employees with at least 1 year of service may request up to 30 days of unpaid leave
- Approval is at the discretion of the department head and HR
- Benefits coverage during unpaid leave is the employee's responsibility after 14 days

### 4. Attendance and Punctuality

#### 4.1 Working Hours
- Standard working hours: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday
- Core hours: 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM (all employees must be available during this window)
- Lunch break: minimum 30 minutes, recommended 1 hour
- Flexible start times (8:00 AM - 10:00 AM) are available with manager approval

#### 4.2 Late Arrivals
- Employees arriving after 10:00 AM without prior notification are considered late
- Three late arrivals in a month trigger a formal discussion with the manager
- Persistent lateness (more than 6 occurrences in a quarter) may result in disciplinary action
- Late arrivals exceeding 2 hours require deduction from annual leave balance or unpaid leave

#### 4.3 Absence Without Notice
- Unexplained absence for 2 consecutive working days is treated as job abandonment
- HR will attempt emergency contact before initiating termination procedures
- No-call no-show incidents are documented in the employee's permanent record

### 5. Leave Request Process
1. Submit leave request through the HR portal (Workday) at least 14 days in advance
2. Manager reviews and approves or denies within 3 business days
3. For sick leave, notify manager via email or Slack by 9:00 AM
4. Submit medical documentation within 48 hours of return for extended sick leave
5. HR tracks all leave balances and provides monthly statements

### 6. Compliance and Violations
- Falsifying leave records or time sheets is grounds for immediate termination
- Pattern of Monday or Friday sick leave will trigger an HR review
- Employees on leave who engage in unauthorized employment will forfeit leave benefits
- All leave records are subject to audit by HR and external compliance teams
