# 2. Company History and Mission

## Our Story

Company Inc. was established in **2014** by a small team of engineers who believed enterprise software should be simpler, faster, and more accessible for organizations of all sizes. What began as a startup operating from a shared coworking space has evolved into a mid-sized technology company employing approximately **500 professionals** across engineering, product development, sales, marketing, customer success, finance, legal, and corporate operations.

During our early years, the company focused primarily on developing cloud-native business automation tools for small businesses. By **2018**, our customer base had expanded internationally, prompting investments in scalable cloud infrastructure, cybersecurity, and customer support operations.

The launch of our second-generation platform in **2021** represented a significant milestone, introducing artificial intelligence capabilities, workflow automation, and enterprise-grade security features. These innovations enabled Company Inc. to serve organizations ranging from emerging startups to Fortune 1000 companies.

Today, our solutions support customers across more than **35 countries**, processing millions of secure business transactions every month while maintaining industry-leading reliability standards.

Although our products continue to evolve, our mission remains centered on solving meaningful business problems through thoughtful engineering and exceptional customer experiences.

# 3. Employment Classifications

To ensure consistent administration of compensation, benefits, scheduling, and employment policies, Company Inc. classifies employees according to applicable employment laws and business requirements.

Employment classification does not guarantee continued employment and does not alter the at-will employment relationship where permitted by applicable law.

---

## Full-Time Employees

Full-time employees are regularly scheduled to work **40 hours per week**.

Eligibility includes:

* Medical insurance
* Dental insurance
* Vision insurance
* 401(k) retirement plan
* Paid Time Off
* Paid holidays
* Life insurance
* Employee Assistance Program
* Tuition reimbursement
* Wellness initiatives

Benefits generally become effective on the **first day of the month following 30 days of employment**, unless otherwise specified by applicable benefit plans.

---

## Part-Time Employees

Part-time employees generally work fewer than **30 hours per week**.

Depending on average scheduled hours and applicable laws, part-time employees may qualify for selected benefits including:

* Paid sick leave
* Retirement savings participation
* Employee discounts
* Limited wellness programs

Eligibility requirements are determined by benefit plan documents.

---

## Temporary Employees

Temporary employees are hired for limited-duration assignments, seasonal projects, or business needs expected to last fewer than **12 months**.

Temporary employment does not guarantee permanent employment.

Temporary employees may not be eligible for Company-sponsored benefit programs unless required by law.

---

## Interns

Company Inc. partners with universities and educational institutions to provide internship opportunities throughout the year.

Internship programs generally last:

* 10 weeks (Summer)
* 12 weeks (Fall)
* 12 weeks (Spring)

Interns receive:

* Structured learning plans
* Assigned mentors
* Project ownership
* Midpoint evaluations
* Final performance reviews

High-performing interns may receive offers for full-time employment following graduation.

---

## Independent Contractors

Independent contractors provide specialized services under written contractual agreements.

Contractors:

* Are not employees.
* Do not participate in employee benefit programs.
* Are responsible for their own taxes.
* Must comply with confidentiality obligations.
* Must follow applicable security requirements when accessing Company systems.

Managers should not engage contractors outside approved procurement processes.

---

## Exempt Employees

Exempt employees are paid on a salary basis and are exempt from overtime requirements under applicable wage and hour laws.

Examples include:

* Software Engineers
* Product Managers
* Finance Managers
* Directors
* Human Resources Business Partners

Exempt employees are expected to fulfill responsibilities necessary to complete assigned work, regardless of weekly hours worked.

---

## Non-Exempt Employees

Non-exempt employees are eligible for overtime compensation in accordance with applicable laws.

Hours worked must be accurately recorded using the Company's approved timekeeping system.

Employees may not work unauthorized overtime.

Managers are responsible for approving overtime before additional hours are worked whenever practical.

---

## Employment Status Changes

Employment classifications may change because of:

* Promotion
* Demotion
* Internal transfer
* Reduction in scheduled hours
* Organizational restructuring
* Business requirements

Human Resources will notify employees of classification changes in writing.

Benefit eligibility may also change following a status modification.

---

## Introductory Employment Period

New employees participate in a **90-day introductory employment period**.

During this period employees receive:

* Regular manager check-ins
* Training milestones
* Performance coaching
* Goal setting
* Feedback sessions

Successful completion of the introductory period does not alter the at-will employment relationship or guarantee continued employment.

---

## Equal Employment Opportunity

Company Inc. provides equal employment opportunities to all qualified applicants and employees.

Employment decisions are based upon:

* Qualifications
* Skills
* Experience
* Performance
* Business needs

Discrimination or harassment based on legally protected characteristics will not be tolerated.

Additional information is provided in **Section 12 – Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion**.

# 4. Onboarding Process

Company Inc. believes that thoughtful onboarding significantly improves employee engagement, productivity, and long-term success.

Our onboarding experience extends beyond the first day and continues throughout the employee's first **90 days**.

---

## Before Your First Day

Prior to your start date, Human Resources will coordinate pre-employment activities including:

* Employment documentation
* Identity verification
* Background screening
* Equipment ordering
* System account creation
* Security credentials
* Payroll enrollment
* Benefits enrollment instructions

Employees should complete all required documentation no later than **five business days** before their official start date whenever possible.

Managers are responsible for ensuring workstations, software licenses, and access permissions are ready before the employee arrives.

---

## First Day Experience

The first day focuses on helping employees become comfortable with the organization.

Typical activities include:

* Welcome breakfast with the hiring team
* HR orientation session
* IT equipment setup
* Security awareness training
* Introduction to company communication platforms
* Facility tour (for office-based employees)
* Team introductions
* Initial manager meeting
* Assignment of onboarding schedule

Employees generally receive:

* Company laptop
* Monitor(s)
* Keyboard
* Mouse
* Headset
* Security key
* Employee identification badge

The estimated value of onboarding equipment ranges from **$2,000 to $3,500** depending on role.

---

## First Week

During the first week, employees begin role-specific training while becoming familiar with internal systems and organizational processes.

Activities may include:

* Department orientation
* Product demonstrations
* Compliance training
* Cybersecurity awareness
* Data privacy education
* Introduction to internal documentation
* Initial project assignments
* Meetings with cross-functional partners

Managers should conduct daily check-ins during the first week to answer questions and provide guidance.

---

## Buddy Program

Every new employee is assigned an onboarding buddy for approximately **60 days**.

The buddy serves as an informal resource for questions regarding:

* Company culture
* Internal tools
* Meeting etiquette
* Communication norms
* Team traditions
* Office logistics
* Professional networking

The buddy relationship complements—not replaces—the manager's coaching responsibilities.

---

## First 30 Days

During the first month employees are expected to:

* Complete mandatory training.
* Meet key stakeholders.
* Understand departmental objectives.
* Become familiar with internal systems.
* Begin contributing to assigned projects.
* Establish performance goals.

Managers conduct a structured **30-day review** to discuss progress, challenges, and development priorities.

---

## First 60 Days

By Day 60, employees should demonstrate increasing independence in their assigned responsibilities.

Focus areas include:

* Deeper product knowledge
* Customer understanding
* Cross-functional collaboration
* Process ownership
* Technical proficiency
* Feedback implementation

Employees are encouraged to provide onboarding feedback to Human Resources to improve future onboarding experiences.

---

## First 90 Days

The first 90 days conclude with a formal introductory review.

Managers evaluate:

* Performance
* Collaboration
* Technical competency
* Communication
* Goal achievement
* Cultural integration

Development plans are established for the following six months.

Employees who require additional coaching may receive extended onboarding support based on business needs.

# 5. Compensation and Payroll

## Compensation Philosophy

Company Inc. is committed to providing competitive, equitable, and performance-driven compensation that attracts, motivates, and retains talented employees. Our compensation strategy is designed to align employee rewards with individual performance, market benchmarks, internal equity, and the company's financial performance.

Salary structures are reviewed annually using compensation surveys from nationally recognized providers. Benchmark analyses consider geographic labor markets, industry trends, job complexity, and specialized skills. The Human Resources and Finance departments collaborate to recommend salary adjustments, which are approved during the annual compensation planning cycle.

Employees may receive compensation in one or more of the following forms:

* Base salary (exempt employees)
* Hourly wages (non-exempt employees)
* Annual performance bonuses
* Sales commissions (where applicable)
* Spot bonuses (see Section 11)
* Equity awards (for eligible positions)
* Referral bonuses
* Overtime compensation for eligible employees

Compensation decisions are made without regard to race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, or any other legally protected characteristic.

---


## 5. Compensation and Payroll (Continued)

### Pay Schedule

Company Inc. operates on a **biweekly payroll schedule**, resulting in **26 pay periods** during a calendar year. Employees are paid every other **Friday**. If a scheduled payday falls on a recognized banking holiday, payroll will generally be processed on the preceding business day.

A payroll calendar is published annually by the Human Resources and Finance departments and is available on the Company's intranet. Employees are encouraged to review the payroll calendar to understand pay period start and end dates, payroll submission deadlines, and expected payment dates.

All compensation is paid in the local currency applicable to the employee's primary work location unless otherwise required by law or specified in an employment agreement.

Employees are responsible for reviewing each paycheck promptly to verify the accuracy of:

* Regular earnings
* Overtime earnings (where applicable)
* Bonuses or commissions
* Paid leave balances
* Tax withholdings
* Benefit deductions
* Retirement contributions
* Other authorized payroll deductions

Any discrepancies should be reported to Payroll within **five (5) business days** after receiving payment to facilitate timely investigation and correction.

---

### Direct Deposit

Company Inc. requires employees to enroll in **electronic direct deposit** whenever legally permissible.

Employees may designate up to **three (3) separate financial accounts** for payroll distribution. For example:

* 70% to primary checking
* 20% to savings
* 10% to investment account

Employees wishing to modify banking information should submit updated details through the Human Resources Information System (HRIS) at least **seven (7) calendar days** before the next payroll processing date.

To protect employees from payroll fraud, changes to banking information require:

* Multi-factor authentication
* Identity verification
* Confirmation email
* Payroll validation before processing

Payroll staff will never request banking credentials through email.

---

### Pay Statements

Electronic pay statements are available through the Company's secure employee self-service portal beginning at **12:00 AM local time on each payday**.

Each pay statement includes:

* Gross earnings
* Federal, state, and local tax withholdings
* Retirement contributions
* Insurance deductions
* Paid Time Off balances
* Year-to-date earnings
* Employer retirement contributions
* Bonus payments
* Overtime earnings
* Reimbursements
* Net pay

Employees should retain pay statements for tax reporting and personal financial records.

Historical pay statements remain available for at least **seven (7) years**.

---

### Overtime Policy

Only employees classified as **non-exempt** under applicable wage and hour laws are eligible for overtime compensation.

Unless local laws provide greater protection, overtime is generally compensated at:

* **1.5 times** the regular hourly rate for hours worked over **40 hours** in a workweek.

Certain jurisdictions may require:

* Daily overtime
* Double-time compensation
* Holiday premium pay

Employees should consult Human Resources regarding location-specific requirements.

Managers must approve overtime before it is worked whenever operationally feasible. However, all authorized or unauthorized hours actually worked must be accurately recorded and compensated in accordance with applicable law.

Repeated unauthorized overtime may result in coaching or disciplinary action but will not result in withholding legally required compensation.

---

### Timekeeping

Non-exempt employees must accurately record all hours worked using the Company's approved electronic timekeeping system.

Time records should include:

* Start time
* Meal periods
* End time
* Overtime hours
* Approved leave
* Business travel hours where applicable

Employees may not:

* Record time for another employee
* Request another employee to record time on their behalf
* Alter time records
* Misrepresent hours worked

Timekeeping falsification is considered serious misconduct.

---

### Salary Reviews

Company Inc. conducts an annual compensation review during the **first quarter of each fiscal year**.

Compensation decisions may consider:

* Individual performance ratings

* Market benchmarking
* Company financial performance
* Promotion eligibility
* Expanded responsibilities
* Geographic pay differentials

Salary adjustments are not automatic and may vary depending on business conditions.

# 6. Benefits Overview

Company Inc. recognizes that a comprehensive benefits program supports employee well-being, financial security, and work-life balance. Accordingly, the Company provides a competitive benefits package designed to meet the diverse needs of employees and their eligible dependents.

Benefit eligibility depends upon employment classification and applicable plan documents. Human Resources administers benefit enrollment, while individual insurance providers administer coverage under the terms of each respective plan.

Unless otherwise specified, benefits generally become effective on the **first day of the month following 30 days of continuous employment**.

Employees receive an annual Benefits Guide during **Open Enrollment**, typically conducted each **October**, with elections becoming effective on **January 1** of the following year.

---

## Medical Insurance

Company Inc. offers multiple medical plan options to accommodate varying healthcare needs.

Employees may generally choose from:

* High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP)
* Preferred Provider Organization (PPO)
* Health Maintenance Organization (HMO) where available

Coverage options include:

* Employee only
* Employee + spouse
* Employee + child(ren)
* Family coverage

The Company contributes approximately **80%** of the employee-only premium and approximately **65%** of dependent coverage premiums.

Covered services typically include:

* Preventive care
* Primary care
* Specialist visits
* Emergency services
* Hospitalization
* Prescription medications
* Mental health treatment
* Maternity care
* Pediatric care

Employees enrolled in the HDHP may also participate in a Health Savings Account (HSA).

---

## Health Savings Account (HSA)

Employees enrolled in the High Deductible Health Plan may contribute pre-tax funds to an HSA.

Company Inc. contributes annually:

* **$750** for employee-only coverage
* **$1,500** for family coverage

Unused HSA balances roll over each year and remain the employee's property.

---

## Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA)

Eligible employees may participate in:

* Healthcare FSA
* Dependent Care FSA
* Limited Purpose FSA

Annual IRS contribution limits apply.

Employees should carefully estimate expected expenses, as unused balances may be forfeited according to plan rules, except where carryover provisions apply.

---

## Dental Insurance

Dental coverage includes preventive, basic, and major restorative services.

Typical coverage includes:

* Preventive exams: **100%**
* Cleanings: **100%**
* X-rays: **100%**
* Fillings: **80%**
* Root canals: **80%**
* Crowns: **50%**
* Orthodontia (eligible dependents): Up to lifetime maximum of **$2,000**

Employees are encouraged to schedule preventive examinations twice each calendar year.

---

## Vision Insurance

Vision benefits include:

* Annual eye examinations
* Prescription eyeglasses
* Contact lenses
* Lens enhancements
* Discounts on LASIK procedures through participating providers

Typical allowances include:

* Eye exam: once every 12 months
* Frames: up to **$200**
* Contact lenses: up to **$175**

---

## Prescription Drug Coverage

Prescription medications are administered through the Company's pharmacy benefit manager.

Employees generally pay:

* Generic medications: **$10 copay**
* Preferred brand medications: **$35 copay**
* Non-preferred brand medications: **$60 copay**
* Specialty medications: Variable based on plan

Mail-order pharmacy services are available for maintenance medications and may reduce long-term prescription costs.

---

## Retirement Savings Plan (401(k))

Company Inc. encourages long-term financial planning through its **401(k) Retirement Savings Plan**.

Eligible employees may contribute pre-tax and Roth after-tax contributions, subject to IRS annual limits.

The Company provides a matching contribution equal to:

* **100% of the first 4%** of eligible compensation contributed by the employee
* **50% of the next 2%** contributed

Employees contributing **6%** of eligible salary therefore receive a maximum Company match equal to **5%** of eligible compensation.

Company matching contributions vest according to the following schedule:

| Years of Service | Vested Percentage |
| ---------------- | ----------------- |
| Less than 1 year | 0%                |
| 1 year           | 25%               |
| 2 years          | 50%               |
| 3 years          | 75%               |
| 4 years or more  | 100%              |

Employees are encouraged to review retirement planning resources available through the plan administrator.

---

## Life Insurance

Company Inc. provides employer-paid Basic Life Insurance equal to **2× annual base salary**, up to a maximum benefit of **$500,000**.

Employees may purchase additional voluntary life insurance for themselves, spouses, and eligible dependents through payroll deductions.

Coverage is subject to insurer underwriting requirements for higher benefit amounts.

---

## Accidental Death and Dismemberment (AD&D)

Eligible employees receive employer-paid AD&D coverage equal to the Basic Life Insurance benefit.

Additional voluntary AD&D coverage is also available during Open Enrollment.

---

## Disability Insurance

Company Inc. provides:

### Short-Term Disability (STD)

* Up to **60% of weekly earnings**
* Maximum benefit period: **26 weeks**

### Long-Term Disability (LTD)

* Up to **60% of monthly earnings**
* Maximum monthly benefit: **$10,000**
* Benefits begin after the applicable elimination period.

Coverage details are governed by the insurance carrier's policy documents.

---

## Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

The Employee Assistance Program offers confidential support services at no cost to employees and eligible household members.

Services include:

* Mental health counseling
* Stress management
* Financial counseling
* Legal consultations
* Family support resources
* Substance abuse referrals
* Childcare and eldercare resources
* Crisis intervention

Employees receive up to **eight (8) confidential counseling sessions per issue per year**.

Use of the EAP is voluntary and confidential.

## 7. Time Off Policies

Company Inc. recognizes that rest, recovery, family responsibilities, and personal well-being are essential components of sustained employee performance.

The Company provides a variety of paid and unpaid leave programs to support employees throughout different stages of their personal and professional lives.

Employees are expected to schedule planned absences responsibly and work collaboratively with managers to minimize operational disruption.

---

### Paid Time Off (PTO)

Eligible full-time employees accrue Paid Time Off (PTO) each pay period.

PTO may be used for:

- Vacation
- Personal appointments
- Family obligations
- Mental wellness
- Personal business
- Short-term illnesses not covered under sick leave policies

The annual PTO accrual schedule is based upon continuous years of service.

| Years of Service | Annual PTO | Approximate Hours |
|-----------------|-----------|------------------|
| Less than 2 years | 15 days | 120 hours |
| 2–4 years | 20 days | 160 hours |
| 5–9 years | 25 days | 200 hours |
| 10+ years | 30 days | 240 hours |

PTO begins accruing on the employee's first day of employment.

Employees may begin using accrued PTO after completing **30 days** of continuous employment unless local laws require otherwise.

---

### PTO Approval

Employees should submit planned PTO requests through the Human Resources Information System (HRIS).

Recommended advance notice:

| Length of Leave | Advance Notice |
|----------------|---------------|
| One day | 3 business days |
| Two to five days | 2 weeks |
| More than one week | 30 calendar days |

Managers will review requests based upon:

- Business requirements
- Project deadlines
- Team coverage
- Previously approved leave

The Company encourages employees to utilize PTO regularly to support work-life balance.

---

### PTO Carryover

Employees may carry over up to **40 hours** of unused PTO into the following calendar year unless prohibited by local law.

Any PTO exceeding the carryover limit that is not used by **December 31** may be forfeited unless otherwise required by applicable legislation.

Certain jurisdictions require payout or unlimited carryover. Local legal requirements take precedence over this policy.

---

### Company Holidays

Company Inc. observes the following paid holidays for eligible employees:

- New Year's Day
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day
- Memorial Day
- Independence Day
- Labor Day
- Thanksgiving Day
- Day after Thanksgiving
- Christmas Eve
- Christmas Day

Additionally, the Company provides:

- One Floating Holiday annually
- One Volunteer Service Day annually

Holiday schedules for international employees may vary according to local customs and legal requirements.

---

### Sick Leave

Eligible employees receive **10 paid sick days (80 hours)** annually.

Sick leave may be used for:

- Personal illness
- Medical appointments
- Preventive healthcare
- Caring for eligible family members
- Mental health treatment

Employees should notify their manager as soon as reasonably possible before the beginning of the scheduled workday.

Medical documentation may be requested for absences exceeding **three consecutive workdays**, where permitted by law.

Unused sick leave is generally not paid upon separation unless required by applicable law.

### Family and Medical Leave

Eligible employees may qualify for protected leave under applicable family and medical leave laws.

Qualifying reasons may include:

- Birth of a child
- Adoption
- Foster care placement
- Serious health condition
- Caring for an immediate family member

- Qualifying military exigencies

- Birth
- Adoption
- Foster placement

### Bereavement Leave

Additional unpaid leave may be approved depending upon travel requirements and individual circumstances.

Managers should exercise compassion and flexibility during these situations.

### Jury Duty

Employees summoned for jury service are encouraged to fulfill their civic responsibilities.

Eligible employees may receive up to **10 paid workdays** of jury duty leave.

Employees should:

- Provide a copy of the summons
- Return to work if dismissed early, where practical

Jury duty compensation received from the court may be retained by the employee unless prohibited by local policy.

### Voting Leave

Company Inc. encourages employees to participate in the democratic process.

The Company supports remote and hybrid work where such arrangements align with business needs, team collaboration, customer requirements, information security standards, and applicable laws.

Remote work is considered a business arrangement rather than an employee entitlement. Approval to work remotely may be granted, modified, or discontinued at the Company's discretion based on operational requirements, performance expectations, organizational changes, or legal obligations.

- Data privacy regulations

Remote employees are expected to maintain regular working hours consistent with their assigned schedules.

- Calendars should accurately reflect availability.
- Working hours must comply with applicable wage and hour laws.

Employees should promptly notify their manager if unexpected circumstances affect availability during scheduled working hours.

Successful remote work depends upon consistent, professional communication.

Employees are expected to:

- Respond to internal messages during normal business hours.

- Keep calendars current.
- Maintain accurate online status indicators.
- Notify teammates of planned absences.

- Weekly one-on-one meetings
- Team meetings
- Quarterly performance discussions
- Regular career development conversations

- Ergonomic chair
- Stable work surface
- Adequate lighting
- Minimal background noise
- Smoke detector
- Surge protector
- Lockable workspace where practical

Employees are responsible for maintaining utilities necessary to perform their work.

Remote employees play an important role in protecting Company information.

Employees must:

- Enable multi-factor authentication.
- Lock computers when unattended.

- Avoid public Wi-Fi unless using the Company VPN.

- Share passwords
- Disable security software
- Install unauthorized applications
- Use personal cloud storage for Company files
- Allow family members to use Company equipment

Violations may result in disciplinary action.

Employees working remotely must ensure confidential discussions cannot be overheard by unauthorized individuals.

Sensitive information should never be:

- Left unattended
- Printed unnecessarily
- Stored on personal devices
- Shared through unauthorized messaging platforms

## 10. Workplace Safety and Ergonomics

### Purpose

Company Inc. is committed to providing a safe, healthy, and secure workplace for all employees, contractors, interns, temporary workers, visitors, and business partners. Workplace safety is a shared responsibility, and every individual is expected to contribute to maintaining an environment that minimizes risks and supports employee well-being.

The Company complies with all applicable occupational health and safety regulations and continually evaluates workplace practices to identify opportunities for improvement. Safety is considered an integral component of operational excellence and is incorporated into daily decision-making, facility planning, equipment procurement, and employee training.

Employees are encouraged to immediately report unsafe conditions, workplace hazards, security concerns, or incidents without fear of retaliation. Prompt reporting enables timely corrective action and helps prevent future accidents.

This policy should be read in conjunction with:

- Section 8 – Remote Work Guidelines
- Company Information Security Policy
- Business Continuity Plan
- Emergency Response Procedures
- Facilities Management Standards

---

### Employee Responsibilities

Every employee has an important role in maintaining workplace safety.

Employees are expected to:

- Follow all published safety procedures.
- Complete mandatory safety training.
- Report unsafe conditions immediately.
- Use Company equipment responsibly.
- Maintain clean and organized workspaces.
- Wear required personal protective equipment (PPE), where applicable.
- Cooperate during emergency drills.
- Report workplace injuries promptly.
- Follow security access procedures.

Employees should never intentionally ignore safety procedures or create conditions that place themselves or others at unnecessary risk.

---

### Management Responsibilities

Managers and supervisors are responsible for fostering a culture of safety within their teams.

Responsibilities include:

- Identifying workplace hazards.
- Ensuring employees complete required training.
- Responding promptly to reported concerns.
- Conducting periodic workplace inspections.
- Supporting ergonomic improvement requests.
- Investigating workplace incidents.
- Communicating emergency procedures.
- Promoting safe work practices.

Managers should address safety concerns as a priority and escalate issues requiring Facilities, Human Resources, or Executive Leadership involvement.

---

### General Office Safety

Although Company Inc. operates primarily in an office and technology environment, workplace hazards may still arise.

Employees should:

- Keep walkways free from obstructions.
- Store heavy objects on lower shelves.
- Avoid overloading electrical outlets.
- Report damaged furniture.
- Clean spills immediately or notify Facilities.
- Keep emergency exits unobstructed.
- Use ladders rather than office furniture when accessing elevated storage.
- Secure loose cables to prevent tripping hazards.

Food and beverages should be handled carefully around electronic equipment to reduce equipment damage and electrical risks.

---

### Emergency Preparedness

Each Company office maintains an Emergency Response Plan.

Employees should familiarize themselves with:

- Emergency exits
- Evacuation routes
- Assembly locations
- Fire extinguisher locations
- First aid stations
- Automated External Defibrillator (AED) locations
- Emergency contact procedures

Emergency evacuation maps are posted throughout Company facilities.

Emergency drills are generally conducted **twice each calendar year**.

Participation is mandatory unless otherwise directed by emergency responders.

---

### Fire Safety

Employees should immediately activate the nearest fire alarm upon discovering a fire or significant smoke condition.

During an evacuation:

- Remain calm.
- Leave belongings behind if necessary.
- Use stairways instead of elevators.
- Assist visitors.
- Proceed directly to the designated assembly area.
- Await further instructions from emergency personnel.

Employees may use portable fire extinguishers only if:

- They have received appropriate training.
- The fire is very small.
- A safe exit path remains available.

Personal safety always takes priority over property protection.

---

### Medical Emergencies

In the event of a medical emergency:

1. Contact emergency services immediately.
2. Notify onsite security or Facilities personnel.
3. Inform the employee's manager when appropriate.
4. Do not move injured individuals unless necessary to prevent additional harm.
5. Follow instructions provided by emergency responders.

Each office maintains appropriately stocked first aid kits that are inspected regularly.

Selected employees receive certified first aid and CPR training.

---

### Workplace Injuries

Employees must report all workplace injuries, regardless of severity, as soon as reasonably possible.

Reporting procedures include:

- Informing the immediate manager.
- Contacting Human Resources.
- Completing an Incident Report within **24 hours** whenever practical.
- Seeking appropriate medical evaluation.

Prompt reporting supports:

- Employee care
- Workers' compensation administration
- Hazard identification
- Regulatory compliance
- Prevention of future incidents

Failure to report injuries promptly may delay benefits or investigations where permitted by law.

---

### Incident Investigation

Following any workplace incident, the Company may conduct an investigation to determine:

- Root cause
- Contributing factors
- Corrective actions
- Preventive measures
- Policy improvements
- Training needs

Investigations are intended to improve workplace safety rather than assign blame whenever possible.

Employees are expected to cooperate fully during investigations.

---

### Workplace Ergonomics

Company Inc. recognizes that proper ergonomics significantly reduces workplace discomfort, repetitive stress injuries, and long-term musculoskeletal disorders.

Employees should maintain workstations that promote:

- Neutral wrist posture
- Proper back support
- Comfortable viewing distance
- Appropriate monitor height
- Adequate leg clearance
- Proper keyboard placement

Employees experiencing discomfort are encouraged to request an ergonomic evaluation before symptoms worsen.

---

### Ergonomic Assessments

Eligible employees may request a workstation assessment through Human Resources or Facilities.

Assessments may evaluate:

- Chair adjustments
- Desk height
- Monitor positioning
- Keyboard placement
- Mouse usage
- Lighting conditions
- Foot support
- Overall workstation layout

Following the assessment, recommendations may include equipment adjustments or replacement furniture.

The Company generally completes ergonomic assessments within **15 business days** of receiving a request.

---

### Ergonomic Equipment

Subject to business approval and demonstrated need, the Company may provide:

- Adjustable office chairs
- Sit-stand desks
- Monitor arms
- Footrests
- Ergonomic keyboards
- Vertical mice
- Wrist supports
- Laptop stands
- Document holders
- Anti-fatigue floor mats

Employees should not independently purchase ergonomic equipment expecting reimbursement without prior approval.

---

### Remote Workspace Safety

Employees working remotely are responsible for maintaining a safe home workspace.

Remote work areas should include:

- Stable work surfaces
- Appropriate lighting
- Functional smoke detectors
- Safe electrical wiring
- Surge protection
- Comfortable seating
- Adequate ventilation
- Minimal trip hazards

Employees should avoid working from locations that compromise productivity, confidentiality, or safety.

The Company may request photographs or virtual workstation assessments to support ergonomic recommendations where permitted by applicable law.

---

### Electrical Safety

Employees should:

- Inspect power cords regularly.
- Avoid damaged electrical equipment.
- Use surge protectors where appropriate.
- Never overload outlets.
- Disconnect malfunctioning equipment immediately.
- Report electrical hazards to Facilities.

Only authorized personnel may perform repairs on Company-owned equipment.

---

### Computer and Display Safety

Employees who spend extended periods using computers should:

- Take short breaks every **60 minutes**.
- Follow the **20-20-20 rule** (every 20 minutes, look at something approximately 20 feet away for at least 20 seconds).
- Adjust monitor brightness appropriately.
- Maintain proper posture.
- Blink regularly to reduce eye strain.

Managers are encouraged to support reasonable break schedules during intensive computer work.

---

### Manual Material Handling

Although heavy lifting is uncommon in most Company roles, employees should follow safe lifting techniques whenever handling equipment or office supplies.

Employees should:

- Lift with their legs rather than their backs.
- Request assistance for heavy objects.
- Use carts when available.
- Avoid twisting while lifting.
- Keep loads close to the body.

Employees should generally avoid lifting objects exceeding **35 pounds (16 kilograms)** without assistance.

---

### Indoor Air Quality

Company Inc. maintains office environments designed to support employee health and comfort.

Facilities Management monitors:

- Ventilation systems
- Air filtration
- Temperature
- Humidity
- Water quality
- Routine maintenance schedules

Employees experiencing concerns regarding indoor environmental quality should notify Facilities promptly.

---

### Workplace Cleanliness

Employees share responsibility for maintaining professional workspaces.

Employees should:

- Dispose of waste appropriately.
- Store food properly.
- Clean workstations regularly.
- Report pest concerns.

- Maintain conference rooms following meetings.

Facilities personnel perform routine cleaning; however, employees are expected to maintain reasonable cleanliness throughout the workday.

# 13. Employee Grievance Procedure

## Purpose

Company Inc. is committed to maintaining a professional, respectful, and collaborative work environment where employees feel comfortable raising workplace concerns without fear of retaliation. Open communication allows concerns to be addressed promptly, supports employee engagement, and contributes to a positive workplace culture.

Employees are encouraged to raise questions, suggestions, or concerns as early as possible. Many workplace issues can be resolved quickly through open discussion before they develop into larger problems.

This procedure applies to concerns involving, but not limited to:

- Workplace conduct
- Interpersonal conflicts
- Policy interpretation
- Performance feedback disputes
- Harassment
- Discrimination
- Retaliation
- Workplace safety concerns
- Ethical concerns
- Violations of Company policy

Certain matters, such as compensation appeals, benefits eligibility, or legal claims, may also be governed by separate Company policies or applicable laws.

This policy should be read together with:

- Section 10 – Workplace Safety and Ergonomics
- Section 12 – Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion
- Company Code of Conduct
- Ethics Hotline Policy

---

## Guiding Principles

Company Inc. administers workplace concerns according to the following principles:

- Fairness
- Respect
- Confidentiality
- Timeliness
- Objectivity
- Non-retaliation
- Documentation
- Consistency

Every concern is evaluated based upon available information and applicable Company policies.

The existence of a grievance does not automatically indicate policy misconduct; each matter will be reviewed independently.

---

## Informal Resolution

Whenever appropriate, employees are encouraged to attempt informal resolution before initiating a formal grievance.

Employees may:

- Discuss concerns directly with the individual involved.
- Request assistance from their manager.
- Consult Human Resources.
- Request facilitated mediation.
- Seek guidance from another appropriate leader.

Informal discussions often resolve issues involving:

- Miscommunication
- Project responsibilities
- Scheduling concerns
- Collaboration challenges
- Work allocation
- Team expectations

Employees are never required to confront another individual directly if they believe doing so would be inappropriate or unsafe.

---

## Manager Responsibilities

Managers are expected to respond promptly and professionally when employees raise concerns.

Managers should:

- Listen objectively.
- Ask clarifying questions.
- Document relevant information.
- Protect confidentiality.
- Avoid retaliation.
- Consult Human Resources when appropriate.
- Escalate serious allegations immediately.

Managers should avoid making promises regarding investigation outcomes before a review has been completed.

---

## Formal Complaint Process

If informal resolution is unsuccessful or inappropriate, employees may submit a formal complaint.

Formal complaints should generally include:

- Description of the concern
- Relevant dates
- Individuals involved
- Witnesses (if any)
- Supporting documentation
- Desired resolution (if known)

Complaints may be submitted through:

- Human Resources
- Ethics Hotline
- Department leadership
- Legal Department (where applicable)

Anonymous reports may be accepted through the Company's confidential reporting system where permitted by law.

---

## Complaint Acknowledgment

Human Resources generally acknowledges receipt of a formal complaint within **three (3) business days**.

Acknowledgment typically includes:

- Confirmation of receipt
- Case reference number
- Assigned investigator
- Initial explanation of next steps
- Estimated investigation timeline

Some matters may require immediate interim actions before a full investigation begins.

---

## Investigation Process

The investigation process seeks to establish relevant facts through objective review.

Investigations may include:

- Employee interviews
- Witness interviews
- Document review
- Email review
- System log analysis
- Policy review
- Security footage review where available
- Consultation with subject matter experts

The Company strives to conduct investigations professionally while respecting the privacy of all participants.

Employees are expected to cooperate honestly throughout the investigation process.

---

## Investigation Timeline

Although timelines vary depending upon complexity, Company Inc. generally aims to complete investigations within:

| Investigation Type | Target Completion |
|-------------------|------------------|
| Routine Workplace Concern | 10–15 business days |
| Harassment Investigation | 15–20 business days |
| Complex Multi-Party Investigation | 30 business days |
| Regulatory or Legal Investigation | As required |

If additional time is necessary, Human Resources will generally provide periodic status updates to the reporting employee where appropriate.

---

## Confidentiality

The Company makes reasonable efforts to maintain confidentiality throughout the grievance process.

Information is shared only with individuals who have a legitimate business need to know.

Absolute confidentiality cannot always be guaranteed because:

- Witnesses may need to be interviewed.
- Legal obligations may require disclosure.
- Corrective action may require management involvement.

Employees participating in investigations are expected to respect confidentiality to preserve the integrity of the process.

---

## Interim Protective Measures

When appropriate, the Company may implement temporary measures during an investigation.

Examples include:

- Temporary reporting changes
- Modified work schedules
- Temporary remote work arrangements
- Administrative leave
- Workspace relocation
- Restricted system access

Interim measures are not disciplinary actions and should not be interpreted as findings regarding the allegations.

---

## Investigation Outcomes

Following completion of the investigation, Human Resources, in consultation with Legal and management where appropriate, will determine whether Company policies were violated.

Possible outcomes include:

- No policy violation identified
- Coaching
- Additional training
- Written warning
- Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
- Suspension
- Reassignment
- Demotion
- Termination of employment
- Other corrective actions

Due to privacy obligations, the Company may not disclose specific disciplinary actions taken regarding another employee.

---

## Appeals Process

Employees who believe their concern was not properly addressed may submit a written appeal within **10 business days** after receiving notification that the investigation has concluded.

Appeals should include:

- Case reference number
- Reason for appeal
- New information, if available
- Specific concerns regarding the investigation

Appeals are generally reviewed by:

- Senior Human Resources leadership
- Legal Department (where appropriate)
- Executive leadership for significant matters

Appeal decisions are generally communicated within **15 business days**.

---

## Protection Against Retaliation

Company Inc. strictly prohibits retaliation against employees who:

- Report concerns in good faith
- Participate in investigations
- Serve as witnesses
- Request workplace accommodations
- Cooperate with regulatory inquiries

Retaliation may include:

- Demotion
- Reduction in responsibilities
- Unjustified negative evaluations
- Threats
- Intimidation
- Exclusion
- Termination motivated by protected activity

Employees who believe retaliation has occurred should immediately notify Human Resources or use the Ethics Hotline.

---

## Record Retention

Investigation records are maintained securely in accordance with Company record retention requirements and applicable legal obligations.

Access is limited to authorized personnel.

Investigation documentation is generally retained for a minimum of **seven (7) years**, unless longer retention is required by law or pending litigation.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Human Resources periodically reviews grievance trends to identify opportunities for organizational improvement.

Review areas may include:

- Workplace culture
- Policy effectiveness
- Manager training
- Employee engagement
- Process improvements

The objective is not only to resolve individual concerns but also to strengthen the workplace for all employees.

## Annual Review Schedule

The Human Resources Department will conduct a comprehensive review of this handbook at least **once every twelve (12) months**.

- Legislative changes
- Organizational restructuring
- Mergers or acquisitions
- Significant operational changes
- Introduction of new employee benefit programs
- Security or compliance requirements
